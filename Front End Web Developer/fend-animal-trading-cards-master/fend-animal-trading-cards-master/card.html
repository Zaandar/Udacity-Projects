Dolphin

Dolphins range in size from the 1.7 m (5.6 ft) long and 50 kg (110 lb) Maui's dolphin to the 9.5 m (31 ft) and 10 t (11 short tons) killer whale.

  • Scientific Name: Delphinidae
  • Average Lenght: 8 feet
  • Average Lifespan: 57 years
  • Habitat: varied

Nearly 40 species of dolphins swim the waters of the world. Most liv in shallow areas of tropical and temperate oceans, and five species live in rivers. Dolphins are carnivores. Fish, squid and crustaceans are included in their list of prey. A 260-pound dolphin eats about 33 pounds of fish a day.
